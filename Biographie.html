Cellist Nella Balog is a major prizewinner of numerous national and international cello competitions. Most recently she won the Gold Medal and 2 special prizes at the David Popper International Cello Competition. She has performed extensively in her native country of Hungary as both a concerto and recital soloist, and has additionally performed across Europe, South-Africa and the USA.

.
.
.

Born in 2002, she studied in the Szent István Conservatory in Budapest with István Varga. Nella has also studied in Bloomington, USA on a full scholarship at the Indiana University Summer String Academy and participated in numerous masterclasses with Csaba Onczay, Susan Moses, Peter Stumpf, and chamber music masterclasses with Sarah Kapustin and the Verona Quartet.

.

In 2019 she was selected at the Cello Akademie Rutesheim to perform as a soloist with the Stuttgarter Kammerorchester, where she additionally studied with Wen-Sinn Yang.

.

Highlights of the last seasons have included first prizes and special prizes at both the Talents for Europe International Competition (Slovakia) and Jan Vychytil International Cello Competition (Czechia), an invitation to the Scandinavian Cello School for a bespoke career advancement residency by Jacob Shaw, and praticipating at the LGT Young Soloists’s South-Africa tour.

.

From the academic year of 2020/21 she’s studying with Wen-Sinn Yang at the Hochschule für Musik und Theater München.

In 2021 Nella Balog will make her concerto debut in Hungary with both the Zuglo Philharmonic Orchestra and the Mendelssohn Chamber Orchestra, in addition to chamber music recitals.
